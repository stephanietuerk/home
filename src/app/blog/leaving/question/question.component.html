There are some things I would never tell people to do—for example, "you absolutely should (or shouldn't) leave academia"—but there is one thing that I tell everyone to do without hesitation and with a lot of conviction:

Talk to anyone you can talk to.

I would recommend starting by asking everyone you know if they know anyone who was in a remotely similar situation/field as yours who has left academia and ask if they could put you in touch. Do not turn down any offered connection / conversation down. Maybe you are like, "I'm a comp lit person and my friend offered to put me in touch with their friend who left academia with a CS PhD and now is at Google—that's not going to be applicable!" Wrong, talk to that person. If someone offers a conversation with someone in an industry you are competely opposed to working in, take them up on their offer.

Why do I think this is so important? My experience is that it is extremely difficult to understand the landscape of jobs, roles, and organizations outside of academia from within it. And if you don't know that things exist, you can't want to do them, apply to them, be knowledgable enough to have a successful interview, and so forth.

If you don't know what you would ask in these kinds of conversations, here are some ideas:

For people who left a similar situation as yours:

  • How did you get interested in your current field?
  • How did you get your first job in your current field?
  • What was the most important thing you did in terms of finding new opporunities?
  • What was the biggest surprise for you in leaving academia to do something else?

For everyone:

  • Can you give me a high-level overview of the landscape of your field/industry? (i.e. a specific non-profit sector, a sector of tech, tech at large, consulting, federal government, think-tanks, etc.) What is the job market like in it?
  • Generally, what roles are in your organization? Particularly, which would be open to someone like me [describe what you think you are good at, any special skills that may be relevant, and what you think you may be good at with training/experience] Are there roles that would be a good fit (i.e. they would hire me, I would find it engaging) for someone like me if I had a bit of additional training and experience that I could realistically get in the next year or two?
  • Which of these roles are roles that exist across your field/industry?
  • Are there any growth/new areas in your industry where there is a lot of demand for employees but no formal educational path to hire from?
  • What do you like about your industry? What do you like about your particular job?
  • What is the worst part of your industry? What is the worst part of your job?
  • Do you know anyone who you think it would be helpful for me to talk with further? (i.e. someone with similar experiences or background to mine)
