Inspired by similar efforts that I'd seen for Literature and History, I wanted to take a look at trends in the Art History job market via the jobs posted on the Academic Jobs Wiki.

This work is intended to help art historians or people considering an advanced degree in the field have a data-driven understanding of the job market. Users can not only view changes in the total number of jobs over time but also explore jobs by subfield, by tenure status and job rank, and by institution.

To no one's surprise, the Art History academic job market is pretty grim in 2020. This year there are 50 jobs posted, while in the 9 years before this, the average number of job listings was 141 per year.

What was slightly surprising to me was that there wasn't a bigger drop off in jobs before 2019.
