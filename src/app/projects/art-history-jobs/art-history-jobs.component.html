In the field of Art History, the Academic Jobs Wiki serves as an informal clearinghouse for academic job postings. Inspired by similar efforts for Literature and History, this project provides an interface for visually exploring aggregated job postings from the Academic Jobs Wiki to facilitate a data-driven understanding of the Art History job market.

Throughout the humanities, there is a collective understanding that academic job markets are "in crisis," but as far as I know, there are no attempts to describe this quantitatively. The data presented are an attempt to facilitate a shared understanding of hiring trends within the field. I hope that this is useful for prospective and current PhD students and faculty alike.

Users can view changes in the total number of jobs over time as well as explore jobs by field, tenure status, job rank, and institution.

Exploring the data

Along with a of jobs by field across time, there are two interactive sections in this project that you can use to explore the data according to your interests:

The section can be used to ask questions about the quantity of jobs. This includes questions such as, "has the number of tenure-track jobs in Global / Non-Western Art increased or decreased over time?" and "how does the share of jobs listed for specific regions compare to that of jobs listed as General / Open?"

The section can be used to ask questions that begin with, "which schools...?" For example, you could use this section to understand which schools have historically hired for a specific field, which schools hire frequently, or which schools only hire for non-tenure track positions.

The source code for data scraping and aggregation is available on GitHub. Additional for aggregation and field imputation are available at the bottom of the page.
