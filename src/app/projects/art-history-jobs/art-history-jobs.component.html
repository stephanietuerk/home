In the field of Art History, the Academic Jobs wiki serves as an informal clearinghouse for academic job postings. Inspired by similar efforts for Literature and History, this project provides an interface for visually exploring aggregated job postings from the Academic Jobs Wiki to facilitate a data-driven understanding of the Art History job market.

Throughout the humanities, there is a collective understanding that academic job markets are "in crisis," but as far as I know, there are no attempts to describe this quantitatively. The data presented are an attempt tp facilitate a shared understanding of hiring trends within the field. I hope that this is useful for prospective and current PhD students and faculty alike.

Users can view changes in the total number of jobs over time as well as explore jobs by subfield, tenure status, job rank, and institution.
