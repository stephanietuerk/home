about

I design and develop applications that transform data into information. This work is informed by my education and experience as an architect, engineer, and historian.

Currently, I lead design and frontend development practices at Mathematica, a quantitative public-policy research firm. (formerly known as Mathematica Policy Research, no relation to Wolfram). Historically, Mathematica has synthesized very very large datasets into large datasets for its clients, and delivered work via long reports with tables and perhaps charts in the appendices. In 2020, I joined a small web development team at the company as the first person to focus on design, and have since focused on building the company's capacity to deliver professional-quality web applications that allow clients to explore the results of the company's data analytics work in an interactive format.

Before entering the world of data visualization in mid-2017, I spent many years in the field of architecture (M.Arch and PhD in History, Theory, and Criticism of Architecture), and before that, majored in both Civil Engineering and Urban Studies as an undergrad. Although data visualization may seem like a departure from that path, in my mind it is much more a synthesis of it. Data visualization, to me, involves drawing together evidence to make an argument in the same way that a historian makes an argument from primary sources, and it allows me to combine my design and analytic sensibilities in a single endeavor that is fundamentally concerned with making information public. I also enjoy that it places me in close proximity to practices that seek to build quantitative understandings of the world, a subject that I have very much enjoyed thinking critically about since taking two very influential courses on the history of measurement, statistics, and probability in society as a PhD student.

My non-work hobbies include running, rock climbing, international travel, watching mostly non-American films, and cooking mostly non-American food :). I am based in Cambridge/Somerville, MA.
