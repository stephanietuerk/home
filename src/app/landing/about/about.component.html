I am a curious individual broadly interested in how knowledge is produced. I am trained as an engineer, designer, and historian, and now design and develop interactive data visualizations and data-driven web applications at Mathematica Policy Research. Before I became involved with web-based information design and production, I spent a number of years in architecture, first as a designer and then as a historian and educator. I am thrilled that my current work allows me to synthesize my analytic and creative abilities while engaging with both a long-standing interest in the role of quantification in society and contemporary modes of knowledge production.

I live in Cambridge, Massachusetts and use she/her pronouns. My last name is pronounced "turk". My favorite free-time activites include rock climbing, running, rehabing my sports-related injury of the month, recreating dishes I ate when I lived in Singapore, attempting to finish a full week's worth of NYT Crosswords, and watching international films.
