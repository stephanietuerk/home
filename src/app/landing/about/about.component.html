about

I am a product engineer who discovers what users need from complex, technical systems and builds interfaces that prioritize intelligibility, utility, and delight. I love translating between quantitative data, language, and visual representation, and developing systematic approaches to both engineering and design challenges. This work draws on my training as an architect, historian, and engineer.

Currently, I lead design and frontend development on high-complexity, data-heavy public-sector applications at Mathematica, a quantitative public policy research firm. I joined in 2019 as the company’s first design-focused hire on the web team. Since then I’ve grown and led the design practice, served as the frontend lead for our most demanding web-based deliverables, mentored both designers and engineers, and led our frontend delivery practice.

My work includes:

  • Architecting frontend systems that model and handle complex, multi-schema, semantically rich data with clarity and precision
  • Designing data visualization and UI component libraries with type-safe APIs that balance developer experience, accessibility, and architectural flexibility
  • Owning the end-to-end experience and delivery of large-scale, public-facing deliverables—shaping structure, flows, language, and visual hierarchy from early-stage client conversations through product launch and ongoing enhancements.

Before moving into data visualization and interface work in 2017, I spent many years in the field of architecture (M.Arch and PhD in the History, Theory, and Criticism of Architecture and Art). My current work designing interfaces, systems, and developer tools builds on that background, facilitating continued inquiry into how to organize evidence for an audience, how to construct meaning from complexity, and how to make that meaning legible through the development of consistent and coherent systems of design.
